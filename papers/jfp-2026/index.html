About this page

This page hosts the supplementary materials for the manuscript above, currently under peer review at the Journal of Financial Planning. The accompanying Excel workbook implements the worked example presented in the paper end-to-end, allowing readers to verify every computation.

What's in the workbook

The workbook constructs the household financial system problem (HFSP) for a representative household, sweeps the decision space using a deterministic optimization, and reports a representative sample of points along the resulting trade-off surface (the objective Pareto-efficient hull, or OPEH). The efficiency of two illustrative submitted plans is then computed relative to that surface.

All decision variables, constraints, formulas, and assumptions are declared in the workbook. The example is intentionally simplified to ensure full reproducibility within a standard spreadsheet environment; production implementations would extend the same framework with stochastic modeling and additional decision domains.
